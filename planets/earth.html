Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun. It's the only known planet in the solar system that's inhabited by living things. Earth's name is derived from old English and German, and simply means "ground". It takes around 365 Earth days to make a full rotation around the sun. Earth rotates on it's axis, completing one rotation every 24 hours, giving Earth a day and night cycle. Earth's physical characteristics are very unique compared to the other planets. It's lively atmosphere is mainly comprised of oxygen and nitrogen. The Moon orbiting around Earth plays a role in it's gravitational interactions, such as tides.
