Venus, enveloped in clouds and christened after a deity of love, is frequently referred to as Earth's counterpart. Yet, upon closer examination, Venus reveals a starkly harsh environment. As our immediate neighboring planet, positioned as the second one from the Sun, Venus boasts surface temperatures so scorching that they can liquefy lead. The atmosphere is immensely dense, obscuring the Sun from the surface and reducing it to a mere blur of light.
