The Sun, a yellow dwarf star, stands at the core of our solar system, aged approximately 4.5 billion years. Spanning around 93 million miles (150 million kilometers) from Earth, it radiates as a luminous sphere of hydrogen and helium. As the sole star within our solar system, its energy is pivotal for sustaining life on our home planet. Devoid of the Sun's radiant energy, life, as we comprehend it, would be unsustainable on Earth.
