The Moon

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite and the fifth largest moon in the Solar System. It's the largest relative to the size of the planet it orbits.

Interesting Fact: The Moon is slowly moving away from Earth, at an average rate of 3.8 cm per year.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in our solar system. It's best known for its prominent ring system, made up of ice, rock, and dust.

Interesting Fact: Saturn's rings are the most extensive ring system of any planet in the Solar System. They are mostly made from chunks of ice and carbonaceous dust.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It is the fourth-largest planet by diameter and the third-largest by mass.

Interesting Fact: Neptune has the strongest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of over 1,200 mph (2,000 km/h).
